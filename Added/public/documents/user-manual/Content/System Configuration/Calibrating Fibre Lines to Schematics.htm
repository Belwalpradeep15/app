Calibrating Fibre Lines to Schematics

You can display a fibre line on a static image of engineering diagram or a map image. The fibre line is calibrated to X Y positions on the selected image.

Note: Any image format supported by Internet browsers (.gif, .jpeg, .jpg, or .png) can be used. Larger images take longer to display in browsers.

To calibrate fibre lines to a schematic

  1. From the Admin menu, select Fibre Lines. The Fibre Lines Admin dialog appears. The line to calibrate must be designated as Section (engineering diagram) display in the Display type drop-down list.
  1. Click Calibrate to Engineering Diagram () to the right of the desired fibre. The Set Engineering Diagram Calibration dialog appears.
  1. Click Choose File, navigate to the desired image file, and then click Open.
  2. Click Upload in the top-right of the Section Calibration window.
  3. Click points along the schematic to define the path the fibre will take. Each point you click results in a blank Fibre reference field appearing in the Diagram Calibration dialog. You must click at least the start and end locations for your fibre. Panoptes can deduce the distance of any intermediary points you subsequently click based on the start and end fibre distance points you provide above.

Generally, reference points are locations where it will be easy to perform a tap test, locations that create a unique signature in the sound field, or locations whose distance has been validated by a survey. Fibre control points can be determined by physically tapping the actual fibre line in fixed locations (every 100 metres, for example) while watching the Sound Field window, or by estimating the distance between known positions. Associate a distance with every control point.

A Diagram Calibration dialog appears on the point you click. The dialog is pinned to the point you clicked on the engineering diagram.

  1. Place your cursor in each of the blank Fibre line calibration points in the Diagram Calibration dialog , type a reference distance along the fibre, and then click Save.

Click Delete () to remove control points.
